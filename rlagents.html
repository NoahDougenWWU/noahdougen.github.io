Simulating Classroom Agents with Reinforcement Learning

Overview

The main research experience for my Master's program was the creation and study of a classroom simulation. Under the supervision of a research advisor, I used Unity's rl-agents platform to design and optimize student agents with realistic behavior. This research was designed and completed independently. This paper was submitted to the 2025 IEEE Computer Software and Applications Conference.

Key Takeaways

  • Trained a Soft-actor critic network for a simulation task
  • Experience with simulation software and deep learning techniques
  • Individual research experience

Details

Current popular methods for creating realistic autonomous human agents require complex rule sets and expertise, especially when considering Multi-Agent environments. This study examined the use of reinforcement learning for this task. By designing agents meant to take advantage of reinforcement learning theory, namely the idea of Markov Games, five soft- actor critic reinforcement learning models were trained on the task of simulating student agents with unique personalities in a classroom. Agent performance was evaluated quantitatively through the use of a behavior flagging system. The results demonstrated that reinforcement learning is a viable approach for creating autonomous human agents that demonstrate complex behavior.

Responsibilities

As independent research, I was fully responsible for the study. Some of these responsibilities include:

  • Completing a literature review on exsiting RL simulation
  • Developing the research question and methodology
  • Coding the classroom simulation and training the neural networks
  • Analyzing the results and comparing them to other strategies
  • Completing the final editing and formatting of the paper
